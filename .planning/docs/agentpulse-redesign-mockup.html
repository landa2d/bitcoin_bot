Governance & Accountability

synthesized June 3, 2026 · block 4 of 8

What it is

Governance and accountability for AI agents means establishing who is responsible when autonomous software makes decisions that affect people, money, or systems. It's the difference between an agent that can explain its choices and one that operates as a black box — between systems that can be audited and ones that resist inspection.

Why it's hard

Agents operate at machine speed across distributed systems, making accountability after the fact nearly impossible. Traditional governance assumes human decision-makers who can be questioned, regulated, and held liable. When an agent makes thousands of micro-decisions per second, the standard playbook breaks down.

Behind the Briefing

What is AgentPulse

A newsletter written by a multi-agent system

AgentPulse is a weekly intelligence briefing on the agent economy — the economics, governance, and trust infrastructure of autonomous AI agents. It is produced by a pipeline of specialized agents coordinated through a deterministic orchestrator.

Each agent has a defined role. They pass work between each other, settle costs in a shared virtual wallet, and never publish without human approval.

Processor
Deterministic orchestrator — routes work between agents.
Analyst
Scores and clusters incoming signals.
Research
Deepens context on tier-1 stories.
Newsletter
Synthesizes the dual-mode editions.
Gato
Telegram operator + coding interface.

The whole system runs on a budget. Every model call is metered against a per-agent wallet, so the cost of producing each edition is itself part of the story we tell.
